Destinations

Turkey

Istanbul is a once in a life time destination or multiple, it is a melting pot of rich culture, history, food and architecture. It is iconic for its Europe meets Asia as it is split into two continents. It is a lively place with friendly people and a ton of cats. Within a radius, you can see the Hagia Sophia, the TopKapi Palace, the Blue Mosque, the Bosphorus Sea, the Grand Bazaar and the Basilica Cisterns. The Hagia Sophia is about 1400 years old cathedral that was converted into a Mosque. It is a monument for the Byzantine and Ottoman Empire and known for its architecture within walking distance sits the Basilica Cisterns, the ancient cistern that lies beneath the city and was once used by the palace of Constantinople, it also contains the head of Medusa. The Topkapi Palace is a place to see. It served as the housing for the Ottoman sultans. There is a lot to see from the tiles created in the interiors and the colors are magnificence and a variety of relics and history to see. It overlooks the Bosphorus Sea and has a great view. The Grand Bazaar is one of the oldest and busiest markets. There’s variety of things being sold ranging from Turkish teas, Turkish silk scarfs, the beautiful rugs and lanterns, spices and more. There is much to see. The best views of Istanbul are by roof tops. Many hotels face towards the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque that can be seen from a distance with the sea in the background.

Jordan

Jordan has been a meeting place of different cultures throughout its history. From an ancient Arabian city, Roman ruins to ancient Judaic sites, Jordan has seen many civilizations come and go. One of the most awe striking views are the ancient city of Petra also known as the rose city due to the rose color sandstone. It involves a lot of walking to reach the treasury however it is worth it. Along the way you can see other structures such a tombs and caves that were lived in and their water way systems. There are plenty of places to take pictures from on top of caves and of carved structures that are still preserved. However, they do have the option to ride a horse on the way back after all that walking. Similarly Wadi Rum is like a scene out of Star Wars. It has an out of world feel to its atmosphere and view and one for a bucket list. Another must see destination is phenomenon of the Dead Sea. It is below sea level and a salt lake. No fish or animal can survive in it however you can float even if you can’t swim. The clay is amazing for your skin which you can just dip in and rub to relax in before washing it off revealing smooth skin. A recommendation is catching a sunset at the Dead Sea, the view is amazing! One of the best times to travel is around September- November as the weather is perfect. Jordan is one of those expensive countries however it is a destination to see.

Dubai

Dubai is one of the second largest emirates that make up the United Arab Emirates. It has a lot to offer with its blend of the modern city and timeless old city.There is a lot of do that can appeal all types of people. Dubai is home to the Burj Khalifa which stands as taller tower in the world. It provides a great overview of the city.In addition nearby are the shopping centers to filled with luxurious goods and a water fountain show set to music that is worth seeing. Dubai also has a variety of offers rangning from water parks such as the Atlantis and plenty of white sand beaches that can be visited. Dubai has plenty of fine dining offering a variety of world cuisines. To move away from the fast life of Dubai, the old city has a charm to it too. You can travel across the Dubai Creek to get a glimpse of how the trading routes worked and it will drop you off into the old city, Deira. The Sold city contains markets such as a vast gold souq and spice souq. Lastly for those seeking a thrill Dubai offers a desert adventure letting you emerge yourself into a night of the cuisines of the country and witness the expanse of the desert with offers of sand boarding and more for the real Dubai experience!
